Division of Psychology and Language Sciences, University College London

Research

I am currently studying for a PhD at University College London on a 4-year Biomedical Sciences program funded by the Medical Research Council. My PhD is supervised by Prof. Essi Viding and Dr. Jonathan Roiser and is based in the Developmental Risk and Resilience Unit.

My research examines the behavioural and neural mechanisms of social cognition and behaviour. I investigate these processes in the typical population and in disorders that are characterised by atypical social information processing, such as psychopathy and autism spectrum disorders.

I use behavioural, questionnaire, transcranial magnetic stimulation, pharmacological, computational modelling and neuroimaging techniques to investigate how processes such as pain, reward and emotion operate in a social and non-social context.
