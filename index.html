Patricia L. Lockwood

Department of Experimental Psychology, University of Oxford

Research

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Experimental Psychology, University of Oxford. I am investigating the mechanisms of social cognition in health and disease with Dr. Molly Crockett, Prof. Masud Husain and Prof. Matthew Rushworth. I previously worked with Prof. Glyn Humphreys.

Before my postdoc, I studied for a PhD at University College London on a 4-year Biomedical Sciences program funded by the Medical Research Council. My PhD was supervised by Prof. Essi Viding and Dr. Jonathan Roiser and was based in the Developmental Risk and Resilience Unit.

My research investigates the psychological and neural mechanisms that underpin how we interact with other people. I use tools borrowed from neuroscience and mathematics to quantify these brain differences precisely and link them to how people behave when they interact with others. This includes using methods such as behavioural paradigms, functional magnetic resonance imaging, brain stimulation and computational modelling. My current work focuses on how we process information about ourselves and other people and how individual differences in social cognition, such as empathy, determine our ability to do this.
